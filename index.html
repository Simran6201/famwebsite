To My Dearest Mumma – The Softest Part of My Strength

Mumma, you are the first person who ever held me. Before I knew what comfort was, I already had it—wrapped in your arms, resting against your heartbeat. You’ve given me a thousand little sacrifices disguised as routines. You skipped your dreams so I could find mine. You fed me love before I even knew hunger.

You are the quiet lullaby that still plays in my head on the hardest days. I hope you know: every time I smile, it’s because of a piece of joy you passed to me. Every time I rise, it’s because of the ground you built beneath me.

I remember that rainy afternoon when you made pakoras just because I was sad. That’s love—pure, warm, and quiet.

5 Quiet Things You Did That Changed Me:

  • Staying awake when I was sick, even if you were tired.
  • Packing my favorite food without asking.
  • Encouraging me when I was too scared to try.
  • Protecting me from your own worries so I’d stay carefree.
  • Saying “I’m fine” when you weren’t—just for me.

“A mother’s love is the heartbeat of the family, often silent, but always present.”

👔 Daddy – The Strength Behind My Courage

Daddy, you may not have said “I love you” with words often, but I’ve heard it a million times in your actions. In your early mornings and late nights. In the way you’d check on me silently while pretending not to worry. In the times you carried burdens silently so I wouldn’t have to.

If Mumma is the heart of the home, you are its spine. You kept us standing even when the winds tried to knock us over.

Lessons I Learned by Watching You

You showed me that true strength isn't about never falling, but about how gracefully you get back up.

Your calm is a fortress. You taught me that the quietest voice often carries the most weight.

Your love is not loud, but it is deep, constant, and unwavering—a foundation I can always rely on.

“My father didn’t tell me how to live; he lived, and let me watch him do it.”

🎮 To My Partner in Crime – My Little Bro

From the moment you arrived, you changed my life from a solo song into a duet of laughter and chaos. You’re the only person who understands our weird family code, the jokes only we laugh at, the games only we can win together.

You’ve been the reason behind so many of my belly laughs, so many “I can’t believe you did that” moments, and so many spontaneous bursts of joy. I hope you know, even when I scolded you or teased you—it was love, dressed up in mischief.

🌷 Tiny Moments That Made Big Memories

A Warm Spoonful

When I was sick, Mumma gave me warm soup with her hands, not just to feed me… but to comfort my soul.

A Steady Hand

Daddy held my bicycle quietly from behind. I thought I was riding on my own—but he never let go until I was ready.

A Midnight Whisper

Bro once whispered, “I love you more than my PS5,” during a game. I never forgot.

A Hidden Smile

Mumma always saved the last sweet for me—even if it was her favorite. She never told me. I just knew.

A Silent Celebration

Daddy didn't say much when I scored well—but that extra mango slice in my plate? That was his applause.

A Comic Hug

One day I cried. Bro gave me a badly drawn comic of us. It was funny. It was love in ink.

An Umbrella of Peace

Mumma stood outside in the rain while I finished my story under her umbrella. She got wet. I didn’t even notice then.

A Pocket Note

Daddy once slipped a note in my exam pouch: “Do your best. I’m proud already.” I carried it in my heart.

A Shared Secret

Bro and I once broke a vase and fixed it with toothpaste. We promised not to tell. I think Ma knew… but never said a word.

A Wordless Prayer

When I left home for the first time, Mumma just touched my forehead, smiled, and went inside. That touch stayed with me longer than words.

A Watched Dream

Daddy quietly saved my childhood drawings in his cupboard. I thought he never noticed. He noticed everything.

A Hug in a Joke

Bro teases me a lot, but when I’m down, he says: “Hey, I’m still your favorite clown, right?”
